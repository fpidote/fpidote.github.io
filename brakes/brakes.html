Brake check FAQs

Regular brake inspections are crucial for vehicle safety. We recommend scheduling an inspection at least every 20,000 kilometres or following your vehicle manufacturer's guidelines. This proactive approach helps identify potential issues early, ensuring your brakes remain reliable and effective.

Pay attention to warning signs such as squeaking or grinding noises, reduced responsiveness, vibrations, or a soft brake pedal. These indicators may suggest worn brake pads, damaged rotors, or other issues. If you notice any of these symptoms, it's advisable to schedule a prompt inspection to address the problem before it escalates.

The duration of a brake repair can vary depending on the nature of the issue. In general, many brake repairs can be completed within a few hours. However, the specific timeline will be provided after our technicians conduct a thorough inspection and assess the extent of the necessary repairs. We prioritize efficiency without compromising on quality.
